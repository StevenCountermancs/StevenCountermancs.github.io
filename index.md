# Steven Counterman

## About Me

I am a Computer Science graduate from DeSales University with a strong focus on
systems programming, developer tooling, software architecture, and educational technology.

My work emphasizes understanding how software behaves beneath abstraction layers, with
particular interest in program execution, memory models, control flow, and machine-level
systems behavior. I enjoy building tools that make complex technical concepts more observable,
understandable, and accessible to learners and developers.

My research project, RosettASM was accepted, presented, and published at PACISE 2026.
The system was designed to help students visualize the relationship between high-level source
code and machine-level execution through interactive assembly and runtime visualization.

In addition to systems-focused tooling and research, I have worked on projects involving
backend systems, infrastructure integration, networking and security architecture, and
full-stack application deployment.

I am especially interested in systems engineering, platform and infrastructure software,
developer experience tooling, and educational systems that improve how people learn and
interact with complex technology.
